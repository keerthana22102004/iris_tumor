WHAT IS IRIS TUMOR, SYMPTOMS AND THE IMPORTANCE OF EARLY DETECTION

An iris tumor is a growth or mass that develops in the colored part of the eye, known as the iris. While many iris tumors are benign (non-cancerous) and may pose little risk, some can be malignant, such as iris melanoma. Symptoms of an iris tumor can include changes in eye color, a visible spot on the iris, distorted or blurry vision, eye pain, or light sensitivity. Early signs may be subtle, like a small, slowly growing spot on the iris. Early detection is crucial, as identifying a malignant tumor early allows for more effective treatment, reducing the risk of the tumor spreading to other parts of the eye or body. Regular eye exams can play a vital role in early diagnosis and intervention.
